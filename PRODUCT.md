# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Finn is for people who start free trials or subscribe to recurring services and want a calmer way to stay ahead of charges without linking email, bank accounts, cards, or any backend account. They use it in short, practical moments: adding a trial from copied text, checking what is about to charge, importing Apple-billed subscriptions, or confirming that a cancellation was handled.

The user context is personal money anxiety at small but irritating scale. Success means the app makes recurring spending visible, editable, and under control without making the user feel watched or upsold.

## Product Purpose

Finn is a subscription and free-trial tracker that takes the stress out of recurring spending and makes the act of managing it genuinely satisfying.

The product exists because most competitors ask users to connect a bank, card, email inbox, or cloud account. Finn's edge is manual, local, intentional capture: the user chooses what to track, the app parses or stores it on device, and notifications warn before a charge happens.

The primary workflow is:

- Add a free trial or subscription manually, from paste/share text, Apple subscription import, or later screenshot OCR.
- See the most urgent upcoming charge first.
- Manage trials and subscriptions in separate mental models.
- Get local reminders before charges.
- Cancel or update entries without noisy gamification.

## Brand Personality

Quiet. Tactile. Warm.

Quiet means low visual noise, restrained color, generous spacing, and no decorative UI. Tactile means haptics, springs, weighted transitions, and numbers that feel satisfying to update. Warm means friendly copy, soft edges, and a fox mascot that adds identity without becoming the product.

The tone should feel like Things 3 polish with Flighty's time-pressure color logic and a quiet fox. The app should feel crafted, native, and trustworthy, not clever for its own sake.

## Anti-references

Do not pull from:

- Rocket Money: too aggressive, salesy, and anxiety-forward.
- Copilot Money: too cold for Finn's warm utility lane.
- Cash App: wrong kind of playful.
- Duolingo: character fun, streaks, and celebration are out of scope.
- Notion: too web-flat and generic.
- Generic Figma Community iOS glass kits: high risk of model-generated visual drift.

Avoid patterns that make the app feel like a full finance dashboard: price-history graphs, spend charts, category-management chrome, over-explained cards, confetti, gamified milestones, jokey mascot copy, and decorative motion.

## Design Principles

1. Keep the app task-first. Visual craft should make charge tracking easier, calmer, and more satisfying, not more performative.
2. Let the next charge lead. Money amounts and dates are the hierarchy, with the most urgent charge getting the strongest focus.
3. Treat manual capture as a strength. The product promise is privacy through user choice, so entry flows should feel fast, honest, and local.
4. Use the fox as warmth, not explanation. If a screen needs the fox to make sense, the fox is doing too much.
5. Prefer earned familiarity. Use native iOS structure, consistent components, and Things 3-like discipline before inventing new affordances.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Finn should meet WCAG AA contrast where applicable and preserve iOS-native accessibility expectations. Primary actions must maintain at least 44pt touch targets, meaningful interactions should provide haptic feedback, and motion must respect Reduce Motion by removing springs, parallax, stagger delays, and decorative loops.

Color cannot be the only urgency cue. Urgency states need readable labels or text alongside semantic colors. The product should support VoiceOver order across onboarding, home, trial management, subscription management, settings, and local-notification flows.
