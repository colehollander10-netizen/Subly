Subly — Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 18, 2026

What Subly does with your data

Subly uses read-only access to your Gmail inbox to detect subscription confirmation emails and free trial notifications. This access is used solely to identify services you're subscribed to and surface trial expiration dates.

What we collect

  • Email metadata (sender, subject, date) from messages matching subscription-related keywords
  • Service names and trial dates extracted from matching emails

What we do NOT collect

  • We do not store your emails
  • We do not transmit your email data to any server
  • We do not sell, share, or monetize your data in any way
  • We do not read emails unrelated to subscriptions

Where your data lives

All data processed by Subly is stored locally on your device. Nothing leaves your phone. There is no Subly server, database, or backend.

Google API data

Subly's use of Google API data complies with the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

Your rights

You can revoke Subly's Gmail access at any time at myaccount.google.com/permissions. Deleting the app removes all locally stored data.
